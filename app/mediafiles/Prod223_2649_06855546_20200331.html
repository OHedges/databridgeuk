TRUSTEES' REPORT (INCLUDING DIRECTORS' REPORT)
FOR THE YEAR ENDED 31 MARCH 2020
- 1 -

The trustees present their report and financial statements for the year ended 31 March 2020.

 

Friends of Castle Wood School is a Charity formed to raise funds; to relieve the needs of children with special needs, their families and carers resident in the City of Coventry and the surrounding area.

The financial statements have been prepared in accordance with the accounting policies set out in note 1 to the financial statements and comply with the charity's governing document, the Companies Act 2006 and “Accounting and Reporting by Charities: Statement of Recommended Practice applicable to charities preparing their accounts in accordance with the Financial Reporting Standard applicable in the UK and Republic of Ireland (FRS 102)” (as amended for accounting periods commencing from 1 January 2016)

Objectives and activities

Castle Wood Out of School Care operates from Castle Wood Broad Spectrum Primary School in the North East of Coventry. A total of 139 pupils attend Castle Wood School and they come from a wide range of cultural and ethnic backgrounds. The children are drawn from across the City.

 

Castle Wood Out of School Care has been providing extended services to primary children aged 3 - 11 years with additional needs across the City of Coventry for over ten years. These services include a summer scheme and a Saturday Club where funding is available. The children we cater for have a wide range of Special Education Needs including profound and multiple learning difficulties, physical disabilities, Downs Syndrome, Autistic Spectrum Disorders, Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder, children with hearing difficulties, visual impairments, mobility issues and a number of children who require medical support. Places at our out of school care provision are available for children who attend Castle Wood School and also all mainstream and special schools across Coventry and the surrounding area.

Our staff are trained to recognise, respect and meet the individual needs of all children in our care. We provide a flexible and personal play environment in a safe, friendly and inclusive environment. Activities include swimming, messy play, art, rebound therapy, sensory experiences, soft play, outdoor play, sports activities, music and cookery.

The trustees have paid due regard to guidance issued by the Charity Commission in deciding what activities the charity should undertake.
Achievements and performance

During the year 2019/20 we were able to run a successful scheme up to May, 2019 as a Saturday club every other week for 50 children. These clubs give the children that attend a safe and secure environment where they can play, learn new skills and benefit from the social opportunities in the inclusive setting. The clubs provide them with routine and consistency and the children's individual needs are always taken into account for each activity. Due to their needs and the level of support that they require our children are unable to access similar schemes in mainstream settings. Children have been able to participate in activities otherwise unavailable to them. We had visits from a Therapony – giving the children the chance to benefit from being close and stroking the pony. The clubs also provide the parents and carers with much needed respite from the challenging caring role required with children with complex needs. The children would otherwise have very limited options of activities that they could attend and would become frustrated resulting in behavior issues. Many of the children are from disadvantaged backgrounds - without these clubs they may not have the opportunity to engage in activities away from home. Siblings are also welcome to attend giving them the opportunity to share their experiences with other children who have siblings with special needs.

 

Children, parents and carers and staff from outside agencies are regularly asked for feedback and evaluation. Observations are completed and documented. Evidence has shown improvements in communication, confidence, behavior, independence and self help skills. Feedback from parents has proved that this is a much needed resource and they value the support that they receive.

Financial review

Funding towards extended schools activities has come from parental fees paid for each session attended by each child.

 

The balance of funds held as at 31st March, 2020 were £23,964. This was an decrease from the previous financial year as donations were returned due to the pending closure of the Charity.

The trustees have assessed the major risks to which the charity is exposed, and are satisfied that systems are in place to mitigate exposure to the major risks.

Our aims for the future would normally be to be able to continue to provide our extended services provision to enable us to meet the increasing demand for this type of service for children with complex additional needs.

However, it is with regret that due to circumstances beyond the Charity's control our services provision ended in May, 2019 and the Charity will be closed once all funds have been distributed. We hope that a new services provision will be started at some point in the future so that the children and their parents are still able to benefit from activities and respite.

Accounting policies
Charity information

Friends of Castle Wood School is a private company limited by guarantee incorporated in England and Wales. The registered office is Castle Wood School, 50 Deedmore Road, Wood End, Coventry, CV2 1FN.

1.1
Accounting convention

The financial statements have been prepared in accordance with the charity's governing document, the Companies Act 2006 and “Accounting and Reporting by Charities: Statement of Recommended Practice applicable to charities preparing their accounts in accordance with the Financial Reporting Standard applicable in the UK and Republic of Ireland (FRS 102)” (as amended for accounting periods commencing from 1 January 2016). The charity is a Public Benefit Entity as defined by FRS 102.

 

The charity has taken advantage of the provisions in the SORP for charities applying FRS 102 Update Bulletin 1 not to prepare a Statement of Cash Flows.

The financial statements are prepared in sterling, which is the functional currency of the charity. Monetary amounts in these financial statements are rounded to the nearest £.

The financial statements have been prepared under the historical cost convention, modified to include the revaluation of freehold properties and to include investment properties and certain financial instruments at fair value. The principal accounting policies adopted are set out below.

1.2
Going concern

At the time of approving the financial statements, the trustees have a reasonable expectation that the charity has adequate resources to continue in operational existence for the foreseeable future. Thus the trustees continue to adopt the going concern basis of accounting in preparing the financial statements.

1.3
Charitable funds
Unrestricted funds are available for use at the discretion of the trustees in furtherance of their charitable objectives unless the funds have been designated for other purposes.

Restricted funds are subject to specific conditions by donors as to how they may be used. The purposes and uses of the restricted funds are set out in the notes to the financial statements.

1.4
Incoming resources
Income is recognised when the charity is legally entitled to it after any performance conditions have been met, the amounts can be measured reliably, and it is probable that income will be received.

Cash donations are recognised on receipt. Other donations are recognised once the charity has been notified of the donation, unless performance conditions require deferral of the amount.

1.5
Resources expended

Basic financial liabilities, including creditors are recognised at transaction price.

1.6
Cash and cash equivalents

Cash and cash equivalents include cash in hand and deposits held at call with banks.

Basic financial assets

Basic financial assets, which include debtors and cash and bank balances are measured at transaction price.
