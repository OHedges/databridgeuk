COMMUNITY INTEREST ANNUAL REPORT

GREEN CURTAIN THEATRE COMMUNITY INTEREST COMPANY

Company Number: 08900693 (England and Wales)

Year Ending: 28 February 2020

Company activities and impact

The company ran several projects during the Financial Year:a) A workshop “Characterisation and Plot”b) Participation in Wandsworth Arts Fringe Festival 2019c) Participation in Hammersmith & Fulham Arts Festival 2019d) Preparation and publishing of recordings of “Leaving” Competition entriese) Planning and preparation for 1920 centenary celebrations in Irelanda) Workshop on Characterisation and plotThis morning workshop was held at the Camden Irish Centre and targeted at those interested in taking up or improving their creative writing, in particular of material for performance by Green Curtain Theatre. It was well and enthusiastically attended with some returning attendees from previous workshops.b) Wandsworth Arts Fringe Festival - May 2019We took short-listed entries from a previous year’s competition, “Shows from the Shows” and brought them to performance ready pieces in an event entitled, “Shows from the Songs of Ireland” as our contribution to the festival. As usual professional actors were engaged and paid for the rehearsals and performances and in a first for the company, a small choir lead the audience in singing the song that each piece referred to before each performance. There were two performances, one at the Fresh Ground venue in Clapham Junction and the second at the Cats Back Fringe Theatre in Battersea. Both were fully attended.b) Hammersmith & Fulham Arts Fringe Festival - June 2019We repeated the event, “Shows from the Songs of Ireland” at one performance at the Hammersmith Irish Centre, again to a full house.In addition, we ran a “Radio Play in a Day” one day workshop with approximately 10 attendees. Here we explained and gave attendees practical experience of producing sound effects required in a typical radio play using the Foley Artist approach. In the afternoon session, we took a script based roughly and comedically on the sci-fi film, “The Day The Earth Stood Still” (which we called “The Day a Spaceman Landed in Hammersmith”). Attendees worked on establishing what effects were needed, preparing them and performing the scripts. We also provided pre-prepared mood music and certain effects that attendees triggered from our laptops to build the performance. We then ran through a “live” performance which we recorded. We subsequently sent out copies of the recording to the attendees for them to hear how they did. It was a very successful event.d) Preparation and publishing of recordings of “Leaving” Competition entriesAs we had started a Podcast service on iTunes and from our own website in the previous year, we decided to use the skills we had gained from this activity to record some of our events for posterity. We recorded the pieces from a previous competition, “Leaving” using the same actors that had performed them live. They are now available from our website.e) Planning and preparation for 1920 centenary celebrations in Ireland2020 is the centenary of the Irish uprising against British Rule and consequently the Irish Government and many Irish organisations are planning commemorative events to take place over the course of 2020 and beyond. A play, “Muriel” has been in preparation to recount the story of Terence Macswiney, Lord Mayor of Cork City who was incarcerated in Brixton Prison in 1920 and subsequently died there on hunger strike. The story is told through the eyes of his wife, Muriel. The plan is to perform it in London, Birmingham and County Cork, Ireland throughout the summer and autumn of 2020. To support this activity we prepared a grant application from the Irish Government Dept of Foreign Affairs and Trade (as at the time of writing, we have not yet learned the outcome of our application and the COVID-19 situation has thrown all plans into uncertainty).

Consultation with stakeholders

Our stakeholders are the volunteers and staff of the Aisling Return to Ireland Project. We meet with them to discuss projects of a theatrical nature that would benefit them financially to allow them to improve their performance to their service users. Any profits generated by the company’s events are donated to Aisling. This year our profits from events were nominal, about £45, and after fixed costs such as insurance we made a loss. So we couldn’t make a donation this year to Aisling.
